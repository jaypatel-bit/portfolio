Building the Data Access Layer for AI Agents

Led the MCP (Model Context Protocol) project — defining scope, acceptance criteria, and competitive positioning for a strategic infrastructure bet. Identified early that the team was handed a solution without a clear problem statement, and worked backward from CPO vision to define the real scope.

Strategy AI Infrastructure Scope Definition
→
The Situation

The team was tasked with building MCP (Model Context Protocol) support — a data access layer that lets AI agents like Claude pull live data from our platform. The CPO saw it as a strategic defensive move, but the project landed on the team as a solution without a clearly defined problem. There were no acceptance criteria, no competitive benchmarks, and ambiguous scope.

What I Did

I synthesized the CPO's strategic vision into a concrete scope document, flagging the gaps between "build MCP" and "ship something users actually want." I defined acceptance criteria by working backward from how AI agents would realistically interact with our data. I coordinated verification testing across Facebook, Facebook Ads, Instagram, and LinkedIn integrations with two engineers, and framed MCP as both a data access layer and a competitive hedge — not just a feature checkbox.

The Outcome

Shipped a working MCP implementation across four major social integrations. More importantly, established a repeatable framework for scoping ambiguous strategic projects — one where the PM's job isn't to wait for clarity, but to create it.

GEO Tracker: AI Search Visibility for Agencies

Led user research and product direction for a new product tracking brand visibility across AI search engines — ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, and others. Designed and ran live user interviews with at-risk agency customers to validate the problem space and shape the roadmap.

User Research New Product AI Search
→
The Situation

AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude) are reshaping how people find brands online — but agencies had zero visibility into whether their clients were showing up in AI-generated answers. This was a greenfield product opportunity with no established playbook.

What I Did

Designed and led the user research program from scratch. Ran live interviews with agency customers, including one at active churn risk, to understand how they thought about AI search visibility. Used their language and mental models to shape the product framing — not just feature specs. Collaborated with the team to define what "tracking brand visibility in AI search" even means when there are no standard metrics yet.

The Outcome

Research directly shaped the GEO Tracker roadmap and positioning. The churn-risk customer stayed. The interviews surfaced that agencies didn't just want data — they wanted a story they could tell their own clients about AI readiness, which reframed the entire product narrative.

Advanced Filtering: Cross-Platform Analytics

Shipped advanced filtering across GA4, Google Search Console, and Google Ads — coordinating a cross-functional launch across engineering, design, and marketing. Focused on the unglamorous but high-impact work of making complex data accessible to non-technical agency users.

Shipped Feature Cross-Functional Analytics
→
The Situation

Agency users were drowning in analytics data from multiple Google platforms but lacked the ability to slice it meaningfully. The existing filtering was basic and didn't work consistently across GA4, Google Search Console, and Google Ads — three platforms with very different data models and API structures.

What I Did

Scoped and led a cross-functional effort across engineering, design, and marketing. The core PM challenge wasn't the UI — it was abstracting three different API filtering models into one intuitive interface that non-technical agency users could actually use. Coordinated the full launch sequence including documentation, in-app messaging, and customer communication.

The Outcome

Shipped a unified advanced filtering experience across all three Google integrations. Reduced the number of support tickets related to "can't find my data" and gave agencies the granular reporting they needed to retain their own clients. The unglamorous infrastructure work that quietly moves retention numbers.

Idea
Never get broken by a third-party API change again. Monitoring that catches breaking changes before your users do.
Problem
